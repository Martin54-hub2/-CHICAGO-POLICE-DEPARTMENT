Department Offices

Organizational structure of the Chicago Police Department

Office of the Superintendent

Superintendent B. Stafford

The Department is led by the Superintendent of Police, who is appointed by the Mayor. The Office of the Superintendent is responsible for critical functions such as planning and implementing the Community Policing Strategy, facilitating and coordinating law enforcement services, and addressing legal and legislative matters.

Bureaus & Divisions

Bureau of Detectives

Investigates major crimes including homicide, robbery, burglary, and financial crimes across the city.

Bureau of Patrol

Responsible for field operations including protection of life and property across all 22 police districts.

Bureau of Internal Affairs

Investigates allegations of misconduct by department members and ensures professional standards.

Bureau of Counter-Terrorism

Coordinates counter-terrorism operations, intelligence gathering, and works with federal agencies.

Education & Training

Provides comprehensive instruction for recruits and continuing education for all active officers.

Community Policing

Implements the Community Policing Strategy, coordinates beat meetings, and strengthens relationships.
