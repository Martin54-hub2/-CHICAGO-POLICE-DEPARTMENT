Use of Force Policies

Constitutional Policing Standards

Policies dictate Department members' ability to use force. Chicago City Police Department is committed to constitutional policing and the protection of civil rights.

Core Principles

Our use of force policies are built on the following core principles:

  • Sanctity of human life
  • De-escalation when possible
  • Proportional response
  • Accountability and transparency
  • Ongoing training and review

Policy Overview

All officers are trained to use only the force necessary to accomplish lawful objectives. We continuously review and update our policies to reflect best practices, legal standards, and community input.

Training & Standards

Officer Training Programs

Every officer undergoes rigorous training in de-escalation, crisis intervention, and community-oriented policing principles.

Academy Training

All recruits complete comprehensive academy training covering:

  • Constitutional law and civil rights
  • De-escalation techniques
  • Crisis intervention
  • Community policing strategies
  • Physical fitness and defensive tactics
  • Firearms training and safety

Continuing Education

Officers participate in ongoing training throughout their careers, including annual recertification and specialized courses in emerging law enforcement practices.
