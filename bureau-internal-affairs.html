Bureau of Internal Affairs

Ensuring integrity and accountability

Our Mission

The Bureau of Internal Affairs (BIA) is responsible for investigating allegations of misconduct by members of the Chicago Police Department. We are committed to maintaining the highest standards of professionalism, integrity, and accountability within the department.

Our mission is to ensure that all CPD members adhere to departmental policies, city ordinances, state laws, and federal regulations. Through fair and thorough investigations, we work to maintain public trust and hold officers accountable when they fall short of these standards.

What We Investigate

The Bureau of Internal Affairs investigates a wide range of allegations, including:

⚖️ Use of Force

Excessive or unnecessary force, improper use of firearms, Taser misuse, and physical abuse.

🚫 Misconduct

Violations of department rules, unprofessional conduct, insubordination, and policy violations.

💼 Corruption

Bribery, theft, embezzlement, fraud, and abuse of authority for personal gain.

🔒 Criminal Conduct

Violations of local, state, or federal law by on-duty or off-duty officers.

👥 Discrimination

Bias-based policing, harassment, and civil rights violations.

📋 Policy Violations

Failure to follow departmental procedures, reporting requirements, and professional standards.

The Investigation Process

1. Complaint Intake

Complaints can be filed in person, by phone, online, or through COPA (Civilian Office of Police Accountability) for allegations of excessive force or serious misconduct.

2. Initial Review

BIA reviews the complaint to determine jurisdiction and assigns the case to an investigator. Cases are categorized based on severity and type of allegation.

3. Investigation

Trained investigators conduct thorough, impartial investigations that may include:

  • Interviews with complainants, witnesses, and accused officers
  • Review of body-worn camera footage, dashcam video, and other evidence
  • Analysis of police reports, radio transmissions, and GPS data
  • Consultation with subject matter experts when needed

4. Findings & Recommendations

Upon completion, investigators present findings to supervisors who determine whether allegations are:

  • Sustained: Evidence supports the allegation
  • Not Sustained: Insufficient evidence to prove or disprove
  • Unfounded: Allegation is false or not supported by facts
  • Exonerated: Incident occurred but was lawful and proper

5. Disciplinary Action

If misconduct is sustained, disciplinary action may range from counseling and retraining to suspension or termination, depending on the severity of the violation.

Transparency & Accountability

The Chicago Police Department is committed to transparency in the disciplinary process:

Public Reporting: BIA publishes annual reports detailing complaint statistics, investigation outcomes, and disciplinary actions taken.
Independent Oversight: COPA provides civilian oversight of police misconduct investigations, ensuring accountability and public trust.
Officer Accountability Database: Information about sustained findings and disciplinary actions is maintained and accessible to the public.

Your Rights as a Complainant

  • You have the right to file a complaint without fear of retaliation
  • You will receive a complaint tracking number and updates on the status of your case
  • You may request that the investigation be conducted by COPA for serious allegations
  • You have the right to representation and support throughout the process
  • All investigations are confidential, and your identity is protected to the extent allowed by law
