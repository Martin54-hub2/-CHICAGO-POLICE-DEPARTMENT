Training Needs Assessment

Building a professional, well-trained police force

Overview

The Chicago Police Department is committed to providing comprehensive, ongoing training to all department members. Our Training Needs Assessment identifies gaps in current training programs, evaluates emerging challenges, and ensures officers have the skills and knowledge necessary to serve the community effectively and safely.

This assessment is conducted annually in collaboration with the Training Division, field supervisors, community stakeholders, and independent oversight bodies to ensure training aligns with best practices and community needs.

Current Training Programs

📚 Recruit Training

6-month academy program covering:

  • Constitutional policing & civil rights
  • De-escalation techniques
  • Use of force policies
  • Emergency vehicle operations
  • Community engagement

🔄 In-Service Training

Annual required training for all sworn personnel:

  • 40 hours of mandatory topics
  • Policy updates & legal changes
  • Scenario-based exercises
  • Officer wellness & mental health

🎯 Specialized Training

Advanced courses for specific assignments:

  • Detective investigations
  • Crisis intervention (CIT)
  • Tactical response
  • Supervision & leadership

Identified Training Gaps

Our assessment has identified the following areas requiring enhanced or new training:

Priority 1: De-Escalation & Crisis Response

While crisis intervention training exists, officers need more frequent scenario-based practice and refresher courses to maintain proficiency in de-escalation techniques.

Priority 2: Implicit Bias & Fair & Impartial Policing

Ongoing training needed to recognize and mitigate unconscious bias in policing decisions, traffic stops, and community interactions.

Priority 3: Technology & Digital Evidence

Officers require more comprehensive training on body-worn cameras, in-car systems, evidence.com usage, and digital evidence preservation.

Priority 4: Community Policing Strategies

Enhanced training on problem-solving models, community partnership building, and proactive crime prevention approaches.

Priority 5: Officer Wellness & Trauma

Expanded mental health resources, peer support training, and trauma-informed approaches to officer well-being.

2026 Training Initiatives

Based on this assessment, the following training programs will be implemented or expanded in 2026:

Enhanced De-Escalation Program

  • Quarterly scenario-based training with role-players
  • Virtual reality simulations for high-stress situations
  • Integration with use of force reviews for continuous improvement

Procedural Justice Training

  • Mandatory training for all officers on respectful interactions
  • Focus on voice, neutrality, respect, and trustworthiness
  • Community feedback integration into training scenarios

Supervisor Development Academy

  • 40-hour leadership program for sergeants and lieutenants
  • Accountability, mentoring, and performance management
  • Early intervention and officer support systems

Technology Proficiency Certification

  • Hands-on training for all digital systems and tools
  • Regular assessments to ensure competency
  • Refresher courses for system updates

Training Metrics & Accountability

The CPD tracks training compliance and effectiveness through:

  • Completion Rates: Monitoring which officers have completed required training
  • Assessment Scores: Evaluating knowledge retention through testing and practical exercises
  • Field Performance: Tracking how training impacts real-world performance (use of force, complaints, community feedback)
  • Officer Feedback: Surveys and focus groups to assess training quality and relevance
  • External Review: Independent Monitor and consent decree oversight of training programs

Community Input

The Training Needs Assessment incorporates feedback from Chicago residents through:

  • Community Advisory Committees
  • Public forums and town halls
  • Complaint and commendation analysis
  • Surveys and focus groups
  • Partnership with community organizations

Community input helps ensure training addresses real concerns and builds the trust necessary for effective policing.

Future Priorities

Looking ahead, the CPD will continue to evolve training programs to meet emerging challenges:

  • Mental health crisis response and co-responder models
  • Cybercrime investigation techniques
  • Emerging technologies (drones, AI, predictive analytics)
  • Constitutional policing and civil rights updates
  • Youth engagement and school safety
  • Domestic violence and trauma-informed responses
