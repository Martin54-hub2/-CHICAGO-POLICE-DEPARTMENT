Bureau of Detectives

Investigating crimes and bringing justice to Chicago

About the Bureau

The Bureau of Detectives is the investigative arm of the Chicago Police Department, responsible for investigating serious crimes including homicides, sexual assaults, robberies, burglaries, and other felonies. Our detectives work tirelessly to solve cases, bring perpetrators to justice, and provide closure to victims and their families.

With specialized units covering every type of major crime, our bureau combines traditional investigative techniques with cutting-edge forensic technology and data analysis to achieve the highest possible clearance rates.

Divisions & Units

Homicide & Sex Crimes

Investigates all homicides, suspicious deaths, and sexual assault cases. Works closely with the Medical Examiner's Office and forensic teams.

Financial Crimes

Handles fraud, embezzlement, identity theft, and other financial crimes affecting Chicago residents and businesses.

Vehicular Hijacking

Dedicated to investigating carjackings and vehicle thefts, working with federal partners to track organized auto theft rings.

Gang Investigation

Focuses on gang-related violence, organized crime, and narcotics trafficking that fuel violent crime in our communities.

Burglary & Theft

Investigates residential and commercial burglaries, thefts, and property crimes throughout the city.

Cyber Crimes

Tackles internet crimes, online exploitation, hacking, and digital evidence recovery in support of investigations.

Detective Training & Qualifications

Becoming a detective in the Chicago Police Department requires:

  • Minimum of 4 years of patrol experience as a sworn officer
  • Successful completion of the Detective's Examination
  • Completion of the Detective Training Academy (8 weeks)
  • Specialized training in interview techniques, evidence collection, and case management
  • Ongoing professional development in forensics, technology, and investigative methods

Technology & Innovation

The Bureau of Detectives leverages modern technology to solve crimes:

Crime Analysis Unit: Uses data analytics and pattern recognition to identify trends, connect cases, and predict criminal activity.
Forensic Services: State-of-the-art DNA analysis, fingerprint databases, ballistics matching, and digital forensics capabilities.
Strategic Decision Support Centers: Real-time information sharing between detectives, patrol units, and partner agencies.

Working with the Community

Solving crimes requires community cooperation. The Bureau of Detectives works closely with Chicago residents through:

  • Anonymous tip lines for reporting information about crimes
  • Community presentations on crime prevention and awareness
  • Victim services and support throughout the investigative process
  • Transparency in major investigations through public updates
  • Partnerships with community organizations and advocacy groups

To report information about a crime: Call the CPD Tip Line at (312) 746-8251 or submit tips online. All tips can remain anonymous.

Statistics & Accountability

The Bureau of Detectives is committed to transparency and accountability. We regularly publish clearance rates, case statistics, and investigative outcomes to keep the public informed about our work.

Recent Performance Metrics:

  • Homicide clearance rate: Ongoing improvement through focused investigative strategies
  • Sexual assault case follow-through: 100% of reported cases assigned to trained detectives
  • Property crime recovery: Increased coordination with regional agencies
  • Cold case unit: Reopening and solving cases using new forensic techniques
