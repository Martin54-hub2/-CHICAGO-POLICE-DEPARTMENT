Freedom of Information Act (FOIA)

The Chicago Police Department is committed to transparency and accountability. Under the Illinois Freedom of Information Act (FOIA), the public has the right to request access to public records maintained by the CPD.

We process FOIA requests in accordance with state law and department policy, balancing the public's right to know with privacy protections, ongoing investigations, and public safety considerations.

What Records Can I Request?

Commonly requested CPD records include:

📋 Police Reports

Incident reports, case reports, and supplemental reports for crimes and incidents you were involved in or witnessed.

📹 Body Camera Footage

Video recordings from officer body-worn cameras, subject to review and redaction for privacy and investigative reasons.

🚔 Arrest Records

Records of arrests, including booking information and charges filed (excluding sealed or expunged records).

🚗 Traffic Crash Reports

Police reports for vehicle accidents and traffic incidents.

📊 Crime Statistics

Aggregate crime data, reports by district or beat, and public safety statistics.

📝 Policies & Procedures

Department policies, general orders, and operational procedures (non-sensitive materials).

Exemptions & Limitations

While the CPD strives for transparency, certain records are exempt from disclosure under FOIA, including:

  • Active Investigations: Records that could interfere with ongoing criminal investigations
  • Personal Privacy: Information that would constitute an unwarranted invasion of personal privacy
  • Law Enforcement Techniques: Confidential investigative methods and security procedures
  • Juvenile Records: Records involving minors (unless legally required to disclose)
  • Sealed or Expunged Records: Court-ordered sealed or expunged criminal records
  • Confidential Sources: Identities of confidential informants and witnesses
  • Attorney-Client Privilege: Communications with legal counsel
Note: If your request contains exempt information, we will provide any portions that can be released while redacting exempt material.

What to Include in Your Request

To help us process your request efficiently, please provide:

  • Specific Description: Clearly describe the records you're seeking (incident dates, locations, case numbers if known)
  • Your Contact Information: Name, address, phone number, and email for updates and delivery
  • Preferred Format: Electronic (PDF) or paper copies
  • Proof of Identity: For certain records (body camera footage, personal information), you may need to verify your identity or relationship to the incident

Processing Timeline & Fees

Response Time

The CPD will respond to your FOIA request within 5 business days with one of the following:

  • Approval and provision of the requested records
  • Denial with a detailed explanation of applicable exemptions
  • Extension notice (up to 5 additional days for voluminous or complex requests)

Fees

FOIA requests may incur fees for:

  • Copies: $0.15 per page for black & white, $0.25 per page for color
  • Certification: $1.00 per document
  • Electronic Media: Actual cost of CD/DVD or flash drive
  • Labor: For requests requiring more than 8 hours of work, labor fees may apply

The first 50 pages of black & white copies are provided free of charge.

Appeals Process

If your FOIA request is denied in whole or in part, you have the right to appeal:

  • Submit a written appeal within 60 days of the denial
  • Address your appeal to the CPD FOIA Officer
  • The CPD will respond to your appeal within 7 business days
  • If still unsatisfied, you may seek review by the Illinois Attorney General's Public Access Counselor or file a lawsuit in circuit court

Frequently Requested Documents

The following records are frequently requested and may already be available online:

  • Crime Statistics: Monthly crime reports by district available on the CPD Data Portal
  • Use of Force Reports: Quarterly summaries published in compliance with consent decree requirements
  • Department Policies: Many general orders and policies are posted on the CPD website
  • Training Materials: Publicly available training curricula and academy information
  • Budget & Financial Reports: Annual budget documents and expenditure reports

Check our website and data portal before filing a FOIA request—you may find what you need immediately!
